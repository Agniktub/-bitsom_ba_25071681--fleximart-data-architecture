# Database Schema Documentation

## Entity-Relationship Description (Text Format)

ENTITY: customers
Purpose: Stores customer information
Attributes:
  - customer_id: Unique identifier (Primary Key)
  - first_name: Customer's first name
  - last_name: Customer's last name
  - email: Customer's email address (unique and not null)
  - phone: Customer's phone number
  - city: Customer's city
  - registration_date: Date when the customer registered
Relationships:
  - One customer can place MANY orders (1:M with orders table)

ENTITY: products
Purpose: Stores product information
Attributes:
  - product_id: Unique identifier (Primary Key)
  - product_name: Name of the product
  - category: Product category
  - price: Product price (decimal)
  - stock_quantity: Available stock (default 0)
Relationships:
  - One product can be part of MANY order_items (1:M with order_items table)

ENTITY: orders
Purpose: Stores order header information
Attributes:
  - order_id: Unique identifier (Primary Key)
  - customer_id: Foreign key referencing customers
  - order_date: Date of the order
  - total_amount: Total amount of the order
  - status: Order status (default 'Pending')
Relationships:
  - One order belongs to one customer (M:1 with customers table)
  - One order can have MANY order_items (1:M with order_items table)

ENTITY: order_items
Purpose: Stores details of items in orders
Attributes:
  - order_item_id: Unique identifier (Primary Key)
  - order_id: Foreign key referencing orders
  - product_id: Foreign key referencing products
  - quantity: Quantity ordered
  - unit_price: Price per unit at time of order
  - subtotal: Subtotal for the item (quantity * unit_price)
Relationships:
  - One order_item belongs to one order (M:1 with orders table)
  - One order_item references one product (M:1 with products table)

## Normalization Explanation
The database design is in 3NF because it eliminates redundancy and ensures data integrity through proper dependency management. Functional dependencies include: in customers, customer_id determines all other attributes; in products, product_id determines product_name, category, price, and stock_quantity; in orders, order_id determines customer_id, order_date, total_amount, and status; in order_items, order_item_id determines order_id, product_id, quantity, unit_price, and subtotal. There are no partial dependencies (non-key attributes depend fully on primary keys) and no transitive dependencies (non-key attributes do not depend on other non-key attributes).

This design avoids update anomalies (e.g., changing a customer's email requires only one update), insert anomalies (new customers or products can be added independently), and delete anomalies (deleting an order does not remove customer or product data). Referential integrity is enforced via foreign keys. (232 words)
